Women Empowerment Schemes


Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP) Scheme :


  • Established Year: 2015

  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao aims to address the declining child sex ratio and promote the education and welfare of girls.

  • It emphasizes the importance of educating girls and empowering them to participate equally in society.

  • The scheme also aims to prevent gender-based discrimination and violence against women.

Eligibility


  • The scheme targets all districts in India, particularly those with low child sex ratios and high rates of female foeticide and infanticide.

  • The family should have a girl child below 10 years of age.

  • There should be a Sukanya Samriddhi Account or SSA which has been opened in any Indian bank, in the name of the girl child in the family.

  • The girl child should be a resident Indian. NRI citizens do not possess eligibility for the BBBP scheme.

Benefits


  • Raises awareness about the importance of educating girls and supporting their development. Provision for a girl child to open an account for future financial security.

  • The account is exempted under the Income Tax Act of 1961, Section 80C, remaining entirely tax-free.

  • Offers a competitive interest rate, allowing withdrawal only when the girl child turns 18. Provides an easy savings facility for daughters facilitated by parents or guardians.

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY):


  • Established Year: 2017

  • PMMVY provides financial assistance to pregnant and lactating mothers to support their health and nutrition needs during pregnancy and childbirth.

  • The scheme aims to reduce maternal and infant mortality rates by ensuring access to proper nutrition, antenatal care, and institutional delivery.

Eligibility


  • Be pregnant or a lactating mother with their first child.

  • Be at least 18 years old.

  • Enroll in the scheme before childbirth.

  • Ensure institutional delivery and child immunization to receive benefits.

Benefits


  • Receive monetary assistance during pregnancy and lactation.

  • Get help with healthcare and nutrition expenses.

  • Enhance the well-being and empowerment of pregnant and lactating mothers.

  • Ensure better health for both mother and child through institutional delivery and immunization.

Mahila Shakti Kendra (MSK)


  • Established Year: 2017

  • Mahila Shakti Kendra aims to empower women through community participation and convergence of various women-centric schemes and programs.

  • It focuses on skill development, capacity building, awareness generation, and support services for women across different age groups and socio-economic backgrounds.

Eligibility


  • Open to women of all ages, including adolescents, adult women, and elderly women, as well as women-headed households.

  • The Mahila Shakti Kendra (MSK) scheme is open to women of all ages residing in rural areas who are willing to participate in empowerment activities.

Benefits


  • Provides access to vocational training, entrepreneurship development, and income-generating activities for women.

  • Offers support services such as counseling, legal aid, healthcare, and nutrition education to enhance the well-being of women.

  • Promotes women's leadership, participation in decision-making, and social empowerment at the grassroots level.

Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA):


  • Established Year: 2016

  • Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan aims to provide free antenatal care to pregnant women, especially those from vulnerable and marginalized communities.

  • The scheme ensures that pregnant women receive essential medical check-ups, diagnostic tests, and specialist consultations to detect and manage high-risk pregnancies.

Eligibility


  • Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan is accessible to pregnant women who are registered for antenatal care.

  • The scheme is open to citizens of India.

  • Women of all age groups can avail of its benefits.

  • It is available to pregnant women from all socio-economic backgrounds.

Benefits


  • Provides pregnant women with free health check-ups, ensuring their well-being

  • Ensures safer pregnancy and childbirth, reducing risks to both mothers and babies.

  • Helps lower maternal and infant mortality rates, improving overall healthcare outcomes.

  • Increases awareness about maternal health and care, empowering women with knowledge for a healthier pregnancy journey.

National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM):


  • Established Year: 2011

  • NRLM aims to alleviate poverty and empower rural women by promoting self-help groups (SHGs) and providing them with access to financial services, livelihood opportunities, and skill development training.

  • The mission focuses on enhancing women's participation in decision-making, income generation activities, and community development initiatives.

Eligibility


  • Open to rural individuals and households.

  • Targeted towards those below the poverty line.

  • Specifically focuses on women's self-help groups.

  • Also accessible to vulnerable and marginalized communities.

Benefits


  • Empowers women to become financially independent and self-reliant through entrepreneurship and income-generating activities.

  • Facilitates access to credit, savings, and insurance services to support women-led enterprises and livelihoods.

  • Strengthens social capital, women's leadership, and community solidarity through collective action and self-help group federations.

One Stop Centre (OSC) Scheme:


  • Established Year: 2015

  • The One Stop Centre scheme provides integrated support services to women affected by violence, including domestic violence, sexual assault, and trafficking.

  • These centres offer emergency medical care, counseling, legal aid, police assistance, shelter, and rehabilitation services to survivors of violence, with a focus on ensuring their safety, well-being, and empowerment.

Eligibility


  • Available to women affected by violence.

  • Open to women of all ages.

  • Accessible regardless of socio-economic status.

  • Provides services to women from all backgrounds.

Benefits


  • Provides immediate and comprehensive support to women survivors of violence, including medical, legal, and psychosocial services.

  • Promotes gender-sensitive response mechanisms and survivor-centric approaches to address violence against women.

  • Facilitates access to justice, rehabilitation, and reintegration services to empower survivors and promote their recovery and rehabilitation.

Stand-Up India Scheme :


  • Established Year: 2016

  • Stand-Up India aims to promote entrepreneurship among women, Scheduled Castes (SCs), and Scheduled Tribes (STs) by facilitating access to credit, mentoring, and support services.

  • The scheme provides bank loans ranging from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore to set up greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors, with at least one-third of the loans earmarked for women entrepreneurs.

Eligibility


  • Open to all sectors of the economy.

  • Women entrepreneurs, along with SCs and STs, are eligible to apply for loans under the Stand-Up India Scheme.

  • Requires the business to be owned and operated by eligible individuals.

Benefits


  • Encourages women's entrepreneurship and economic self-reliance through access to finance and business opportunities.

  • Promotes inclusive growth and social empowerment by facilitating access to credit and support services for marginalized communities.

  • Fosters job creation, innovation, and economic development through the promotion of small and medium enterprises (SMEs) led by women and other underprivileged groups.
